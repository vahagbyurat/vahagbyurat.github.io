Philosophy

I have been training Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu for over 7 years and have competed white through purple belt divisions. I train to incrementally rid fear from life by facing it in its physical and primal form. Although there will always be fear in life, it should not undermine purpose.

The mental clarity and feelings of happiness that follow a hard training session are nature's gift for letting the human body free to move in the way it was intended. In an artistic sense, we can view ourselves as the canvas in this art form, and the stress of physical combat as the paintbrush.

The famous Michelangelo believed the sculptor was a tool of God, not creating but simply revealing the powerful figures already contained in the marble. Michelangelo's task was only to chip away the excess, to reveal.[1] The same can be said to the potential of our own bodies and abilities as athletes and practitioners; just chipping away and removing the excess, which doesn’t work for us, to reveal our game and potential black belt version of ourselves.

Reference

  1. Michelangelo's Prisoners or Slaves, accademia.org

Book a private lesson

*Due to my busy schedule, sorry, I am no longer giving free lessons. :(

For San Fernando Valley (Los Angeles) locals, I’m offering a free 1-hour private lesson in either gi or nogi. I still love helping people fall in love with the art/sport, so feel free to reach out and we can work something out.

Deciding to try something like jiu jitsu for the first time is challenging, and joining a gym can be intimidating. For those more comfortable working 1-on-1, I love working with beginners, and hopefully helping you dip your toes in the waters that can change your life for the better! I’m also more than happy to be your buddy to a local gym for the first time, so you don’t feel as intimidated during your first class. Don’t hesitate to ask! And of course I’m biased, but you can’t go wrong with training at Subconscious BJJ if you are close to the North Hollywood area!

I love helping those struggling with situations I have answers to; it motivates me to find more answers to then be able to help more people. I have a weird, fun, and wacky style, but also a firm grasp of concepts and fundamentals, so for beginners I can go over my methods, and for slightly more advanced practitioners, I can go over my madness. ;)
